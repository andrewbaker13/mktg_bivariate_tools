    <p style="margin: 0;">A smartphone manufacturer wants to understand which product attributes drive purchase intent. They're considering launching a new model and need data on customer preferences to optimize the design and pricing strategy.</p>

  <p><strong>Study Design:</strong></p>
  <ul style="margin-left: 1.5rem; line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Respondents:</strong> 150 consumers recruited from a panel, representative of the target market (ages 18-55, smartphone owners)</li>
    <li><strong>Tasks:</strong> Each respondent viewed 12 choice scenarios</li>
    <li><strong>Alternatives per task:</strong> 3 smartphone options (labeled A, B, C) + "None" option</li>
    <li><strong>Competitor products:</strong> Two leading competitor smartphones (iPhone, Samsung) appeared in some tasks alongside experimental alternatives</li>
  </ul>

    <li><strong>Screen size:</strong> 5.5", 6.0", 6.5"</li>
    <li><strong>Storage:</strong> 64GB, 128GB, 256GB</li>
    <li><strong>Battery life:</strong> 12 hours, 18 hours, 24 hours</li>
    <li><strong>Camera quality:</strong> Standard (12MP), Enhanced (24MP), Professional (48MP with AI)</li>
    <li><strong>Price:</strong> <span style="color: #2a7de1; font-weight: bold;">$499, $599, $699, $799, $899</span></li>
  </ul>

  </ul>
